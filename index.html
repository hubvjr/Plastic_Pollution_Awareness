Ocean Plastic Pollution by Numbers

~11 million

Tonnes of plastic enter the ocean each year (UNEP estimate).

19–23 million

Tonnes of plastic waste leak into aquatic ecosystems yearly (rivers, lakes, seas).

2060

OECD warns global plastic waste could nearly triple by 2060 without stronger policies.

Real Incident Spotlight

X-Press Pearl (Sri Lanka) – Plastic Pellet Spill

In 2021, the container ship MV X-Press Pearl caught fire and sank off Sri Lanka, releasing chemicals and plastic pellets (“nurdles”). In 2025, Sri Lanka’s Supreme Court ordered ship owners/operators to pay major compensation for the marine pollution.

Read sources in Resources
Why it matters

Scientific studies on the spill highlight how plastic pellets can contaminate coastlines and harm marine ecosystems. This shows plastic pollution is not only “litter”—it can become a national environmental disaster.
