Causes & Effects of Plastic Pollution

Single-use Plastics

Plastic bags, bottles, and packaging often end up in rivers and oceans due to improper disposal.

Harm to Marine Life

Marine animals ingest or become entangled in plastic, causing injury or death. Microplastics also enter the food chain.

Microplastics

Plastic breaks into tiny pieces that can be eaten by fish and move through the food chain.

Leakage from land to sea

Mismanaged waste on land is transported into rivers and oceans—UNEP estimates 19–23 million tonnes leak into aquatic ecosystems yearly.

Disaster-scale pollution

Ship accidents can release plastic pellets (“nurdles”) that blanket beaches and threaten wildlife (example: Sri Lanka X-Press Pearl incident).
